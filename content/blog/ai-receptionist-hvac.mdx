## Emergency Call Handling

HVAC emergencies can't wait for a callback. A gas leak at midnight. A furnace failure when it's below freezing. Carbon monoxide detector going off. These are scenarios where seconds matter.

AI receptionists designed for HVAC are programmed to detect emergency keywords: "gas leak," "no heat," "carbon monoxide," "flooding," "pipe burst," "smell gas," and similar urgent phrases.

The moment one of these keywords is detected, the system immediately attempts to transfer the call to your phone. If you answer, great — you handle it directly. If you don't answer (maybe you're asleep, or on another call), the AI does several things simultaneously:

1. Sends you an urgent SMS and email notification with "EMERGENCY CALL" in the subject line
2. Takes the caller's information, address, and detailed description of the emergency
3. Reassures the caller that you'll receive the urgent notification and call them back within minutes
4. Provides safety instructions if appropriate (e.g., "If you smell gas, evacuate the home immediately and call 911")

Your phone buzzes with the urgent notification. You call the customer back within 2-3 minutes. Crisis handled, customer impressed by your responsiveness, and you've just earned a loyal customer for life.

This is the #1 feature HVAC owners care about when evaluating AI receptionists. Emergency handling can't fail. The AI has to get it right every time. That's why keyword detection combined with instant transfer and multi-channel notifications is built into the core system.

If a caller says "gas leak," "smell gas," or similar emergency phrases, the AI immediately attempts to transfer the call to your phone.
